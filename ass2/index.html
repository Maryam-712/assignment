Muhammad Ali Jinnah born Mahomedali Jinnahbhai; (25th December 1876 - 11th September 1948) was a barrister, politician and the founder of Pakistan. Jinnah served as the leader of the Muslim League from 1913 until the inception of Pakistan on 14 August 1947, and then as the Dominion of Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death. He is revered in Pakistan as the Quaid-e-Azam (Great Leader) and Baba-e-Qaum (Father of the Nation ).
his fellow barristers described him, saying:

He was what God made him, a great pleader. He had a sixth sense: he could see around corners. That is where his talents lay ... he was a very clear thinker ... But he drove his points home—points chosen with exquisite selection—slow delivery, word by word.
